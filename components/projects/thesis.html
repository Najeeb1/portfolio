Spatiotemporal Learning Thesis

Completed: 2025
Technologies & concepts: CNN, RNN, Transformers, ConvLSTM, PredRNN, ViT, Temporal Attention Unit (TAU), MLP

Project Overview

This Thesis explores the application of machine learning in predicting spatiotemporal patterns. These patterns are observed in many natural phenomena, such as traffic, weather, human movement, and brain development, where objects with spatial properties are moving or changing over time. The research primarily focuses on video prediction, aiming to model and predict the future state of moving objects using spatiotemporal data. We compare different state-of-the-art methods and propose a new, simpler, yet effective CNN-based model for video prediction.

Key Features

  • Exploring the concept of spatiotemporal learning and its applications in real-world scenarios
  • In-depth study of machine learning models such as CNN, RNN, and Transformers for spatiotemporal data
  • Implementing ConvLSTM and PredRNN for spatiotemporal correlation capture
  • Introducing the Temporal Attention Unit (TAU) to improve computational efficiency in spatiotemporal learning tasks
  • Proposing SimVP, a simpler CNN-based model for video prediction
  • Comparing the performance of different models on standard spatiotemporal prediction benchmarks

Technical Implementation

The project explores various machine learning techniques to model spatiotemporal data, focusing on video prediction. The core methodology involves CNN, RNN, and Transformer-based architectures, such as ConvLSTM and PredRNN, that capture spatiotemporal dependencies. We also propose SimVP, a simpler CNN-based model, which avoids the complexity of traditional RNNs and attention mechanisms while achieving comparable performance. The Temporal Attention Unit (TAU) was introduced to optimize temporal learning, making it more computationally efficient. Key technical features include:

  • Building video prediction models using ConvLSTM and PredRNN
  • Developing SimVP, a CNN-based framework for video prediction
  • Introducing the Temporal Attention Unit (TAU) for efficient temporal dependency learning
  • Comparing CNN-based models with traditional RNN and Transformer-based architectures
  • Optimizing spatiotemporal learning processes for video prediction tasks

Project Challenges

During the development process, several challenges were encountered, including:

  • Designing efficient spatiotemporal models for video prediction
  • Balancing the complexity and performance of machine learning models
  • Ensuring accurate prediction of dynamic and chaotic spatiotemporal data
  • Improving computational efficiency without compromising model accuracy

Future Improvements

Future enhancements for the project could include:

  • Integrating additional spatiotemporal prediction models to improve performance
  • Exploring the potential of combining CNN-based models with attention mechanisms for further optimization
  • Expanding the model to handle more complex video prediction tasks with higher accuracy
  • Improving the explainability of the models used in spatiotemporal learning
